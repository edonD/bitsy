# Bitsy Go-To-Market Strategy

## One-Line Strategy

Bitsy starts as a done-for-you AI visibility report for B2B SaaS teams, then becomes software after we learn which parts customers repeatedly pay for.

The first product is not a dashboard.

The first product is a clear answer to this question:

> When buyers ask AI tools who to choose, do they recommend you or your competitors, and what should you fix first?

## The Bet

AI search visibility is becoming a real marketing concern, but the market is already crowded with tracking tools.

Bitsy should not compete as another visibility dashboard. The sharper wedge is the fix loop:

1. Measure buyer questions.
2. Find where competitors win.
3. Explain the gap in plain language.
4. Give the customer a short fix list.
5. Rerun later and show what changed.

That is the product.

## What We Are Not Selling First

Do not lead with:

- XGBoost
- surrogate model
- AI A/B testing
- exact lift prediction
- SHAP
- causal proof
- daily automation
- generic GEO platform

Those either create skepticism or sound like every other tool.

Lead with the customer problem:

> Your buyers are asking AI tools for recommendations. Bitsy shows whether those tools recommend you, who beats you, and what to fix first.

## Ideal First Customer

Start with B2B SaaS companies.

Best fit:

- 10-200 employees
- has a website with product, pricing, blog, docs, or comparison pages
- sells into a defined category
- has 3-8 known competitors
- already cares about SEO, content, or demand generation
- has high-intent buyer questions like "best X tool", "X vs Y", "alternatives to X"

Target roles:

- founder
- head of marketing
- product marketer
- content lead
- SEO lead

Avoid for now:

- local businesses
- e-commerce
- massive enterprise
- agencies with many clients
- teams that only want brand monitoring
- companies without clear competitors

## First Offer

Name:

**AI Visibility Fix List**

Promise:

> We run your most important buyer questions through ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini. You get a simple report showing where you appear, where competitors win, and the three fixes to make first.

Deliverables:

1. Buyer question set: 10-20 questions.
2. Competitor set: 3-5 competitors.
3. AI answer results across ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini.
4. Visibility table: who appears, where, and how often.
5. Gap analysis: which competitors are winning and why.
6. Fix list: 3 concrete actions.
7. Before/after rerun: repeat the same questions after 14-30 days.

The customer should be able to forward the report internally.

## First Report Format

Keep the report short. The customer should understand it in 5 minutes.

Sections:

1. Executive Summary
   - Are you visible?
   - Who beats you?
   - What should you fix first?

2. Buyer Questions Tested
   - List the exact questions.

3. AI Results
   - ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini.
   - Mentioned or not mentioned.
   - Position when mentioned.
   - Sentiment if useful.

4. Competitor Winners
   - Which brands appear most often.
   - Which answer types they win.

5. Why They Win
   - Better comparison page.
   - More proof.
   - Clearer positioning.
   - More cited third-party sources.
   - Better category language.

6. Fix List
   - Fix 1: highest-impact page/edit.
   - Fix 2: proof/source gap.
   - Fix 3: competitor comparison or answer page.

7. Rerun Plan
   - What to change.
   - When to rerun.
   - What counts as progress.

## Pricing Tests

Start with simple pricing.

Free teaser:

- 3 buyer questions
- 3 competitors
- 1-page summary
- goal: get calls, not revenue

Paid pilot:

- $500-$1,500 one-time
- full AI Visibility Fix List
- includes one rerun after 14-30 days

Monthly:

- $300-$1,000/month
- monthly rerun
- updated fix list
- saved history

Do not sell annual contracts yet. Learn first.

## Positioning

Primary message:

> Bitsy shows why AI recommends competitors instead of you, then gives you a clear fix list.

Alternate messages to test:

1. "Find out if ChatGPT recommends you or your competitors."
2. "See where your SaaS brand is missing from AI answers."
3. "Get a fix list for winning more AI recommendations."
4. "Turn AI visibility gaps into pages, edits, and sources to ship."

Avoid:

- "GEO platform"
- "AI search visibility engine"
- "AI A/B testing"
- "predictive digital twin"

Those are interesting later, but too abstract for first sales.

## Outreach Strategy

Use founder-led outreach.

Target list:

- 100 B2B SaaS companies
- categories with active comparison/search behavior
- examples: CRM, project management, devtools, HR software, support software, marketing tools, analytics tools, security tools

Find companies where:

- they have obvious competitors
- they have content pages
- they are not the category leader
- they would care if ChatGPT recommended someone else

Simple outreach message:

> Hey {Name}, I’m building Bitsy.
>
> It checks whether ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini recommend your product when buyers ask category questions like "{example question}".
>
> I ran a small sample for {Company}. You showed up in {x}/3 tools, but {Competitor} appeared more often.
>
> Want me to send the 1-page version?

Follow-up:

> The useful part is not the score. It is the fix list: which page, claim, or source would likely help you show up more often.
>
> Happy to send the sample if useful.

## 30-Day Execution Plan

### Week 1: Build the Manual Offer

Goal: create one sharp, sellable report.

Tasks:

- Choose one SaaS category.
- Pick 10 target companies.
- Pick 10 buyer questions.
- Run Bitsy manually for 3 companies.
- Build a 1-page sample report.
- Write the outreach message.

Success metric:

- 3 sample reports created.
- 30 outreach messages sent.

### Week 2: Get Calls

Goal: get real reactions from potential customers.

Tasks:

- Send 50 more targeted messages.
- Offer a free 3-question teaser.
- Book calls with interested teams.
- Ask what they would change based on the report.
- Ask what they would pay for monthly tracking.

Success metric:

- 5 calls.
- 3 free teaser reports delivered.
- 1 paid pilot requested or seriously discussed.

### Week 3: Sell Paid Pilots

Goal: convert interest into money.

Tasks:

- Offer the full AI Visibility Fix List.
- Price between $500 and $1,500.
- Include one rerun.
- Keep scope tight.
- Do not customize too much.

Success metric:

- 1-3 paid pilots.

### Week 4: Deliver and Learn

Goal: learn what should become software.

Tasks:

- Deliver reports.
- Watch what customers read, ignore, and forward.
- Ask which fix they would actually ship.
- Ask if they want monthly reruns.
- Build a list of repeated report sections.

Success metric:

- at least one customer wants a rerun.
- at least one customer sends the report internally.
- clear signal on the most valuable section.

## What To Automate First

Only automate repeated pain.

Likely automation order:

1. report generation
2. competitor/result table
3. raw answer viewer
4. fix list builder
5. saved runs
6. before/after comparison
7. scheduled reruns
8. customer dashboard

Do not automate billing, teams, permissions, enterprise features, or advanced model diagnostics until people pay for the report.

## Product Roadmap From Manual To SaaS

Phase 1: Manual report

- run collection manually
- create report by hand
- sell paid pilots

Phase 2: Assisted report

- Bitsy generates most of the report
- founder edits before sending
- reruns are easy

Phase 3: Customer portal

- customers view reports
- save runs
- compare before/after
- download PDF

Phase 4: Monitoring product

- scheduled runs
- alerts
- team access
- monthly subscription

## Risks

Risk: Customers do not believe AI visibility matters.

Response: show their actual competitor appearing in AI answers.

Risk: Customers like the report but do not act.

Response: make the fix list more concrete: page title, outline, paragraph, source target.

Risk: The model predictions are not trusted.

Response: do not lead with predictions. Lead with observed gaps and before/after reruns.

Risk: Competitors have more features.

Response: stay narrower. Sell "what to fix first" for SaaS teams, not broad monitoring.

Risk: Reports take too long manually.

Response: automate only the repeated report sections after 5-10 deliveries.

## Video Script Direction

Core hook:

> Your buyers are asking ChatGPT who to buy from. Do you know if it recommends you or your competitor?

Video structure:

1. Show a buyer asking: "best tools for testing AI answers."
2. AI recommends competitors.
3. Founder realizes they are invisible.
4. Bitsy runs the same question across ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini.
5. Bitsy shows the competitor gap.
6. Bitsy gives a fix list.
7. Customer ships one fix.
8. Bitsy reruns the same questions later.
9. End: "Know where AI recommends you. Know what to fix next."

Tone:

- plain
- direct
- no jargon
- no model talk
- no inflated promise
