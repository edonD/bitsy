# Bitsy Market Research And Next Steps

## Research Snapshot

### Buyer Behavior

B2B software buyers are already using AI tools during research.

G2 reported in April 2026 that 51% of B2B software buyers now start research with an AI chatbot more often than Google, and 71% rely on AI chatbots at some point in software research. G2 also says AI chatbots are now a major influence on vendor shortlists.

Source: https://company.g2.com/news/g2-research-the-answer-economy

Implication for Bitsy:

The customer pain is real. SaaS companies need to know whether AI tools recommend them before the buyer reaches their website.

### Competitor Landscape

Peec AI sells AI search analytics for marketing teams and agencies. Its current pricing page positions around prompt tracking, 3 included models, daily tracking, unlimited users, projects, and agency workflows.

Source: https://peec.ai/pricing

AthenaHQ positions around tracking and improving AI search, with self-serve pricing, credits, and support for major AI tools including ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity, AI Overviews, Copilot, and Grok.

Source: https://athenahq.ai/

Profound is enterprise-oriented. It emphasizes visibility, sentiment, citations, trends, platform comparisons, real front-end answer capture, and enterprise security.

Source: https://www.tryprofound.com/features/answer-engine-insights

Promptwatch is already close to the full-loop story. It sells monitoring, citation analysis, content agents, generated articles, content gap analysis, and crawler logs. Pricing starts at $99/month and goes up by prompts, websites, and features.

Sources:

- https://promptwatch.com/pricing
- https://promptwatch.com/content-agents

Implication for Bitsy:

Bitsy cannot win by saying "we track AI visibility." That is already crowded.

Bitsy must win by being simpler and more concrete:

> Here are the buyer questions where competitors beat you. Here are the three fixes to ship first. Here is what changed after the rerun.

## Strategic Conclusion

The market exists, but the software category is ahead of customer understanding.

Most prospects do not yet know exactly what they need. A self-serve dashboard will be harder to sell than a concrete report.

Therefore:

> Sell the report first. Build the SaaS second.

## First Market Wedge

### Primary Wedge

B2B SaaS companies with comparison-driven buying.

Good categories:

1. AI customer support / helpdesk software
2. sales automation / AI SDR software
3. project management software
4. product analytics software
5. devtools / observability software

### Recommended First Category

Start with **AI customer support and helpdesk SaaS**.

Why:

- buyers ask clear recommendation questions
- there are many competitors
- category pages and comparison pages matter
- companies can act quickly on content fixes
- buyer questions are easy to write

Example competitors in this category:

- Intercom
- Zendesk
- Help Scout
- Freshdesk
- Gorgias
- Tidio
- Crisp
- Kustomer

Do not target Intercom or Zendesk first. They are too big.

Target smaller challengers that need visibility against leaders.

## First Report Template

### Page 1: Executive Summary

- Visibility result: visible / weak / missing
- Top competitor winner
- Biggest gap
- Top 3 fixes

### Page 2: Buyer Questions Tested

List all questions.

Example:

- "best AI customer support tools for SaaS"
- "best Zendesk alternatives for startups"
- "Intercom vs Help Scout for SaaS support"
- "best chatbot for customer support"
- "customer support software with AI agent"

### Page 3: AI Answer Results

Table:

- question
- ChatGPT result
- Claude result
- Gemini result
- target mentioned?
- winning competitor

### Page 4: Competitor Gap

Explain why competitors won:

- they have clearer comparison pages
- they appear on review/list sites
- they have stronger category language
- they have better proof points
- they have more third-party citations

### Page 5: Fix List

Three fixes only.

Each fix should include:

- what to ship
- why it matters
- where to publish it
- what to measure on the rerun

## First 14 Days

### Day 1: Pick The Test Category

Use AI customer support / helpdesk SaaS.

Create a list of 30 companies:

- 10 small challengers
- 10 mid-market competitors
- 10 category leaders for benchmarking

### Day 2: Build The Buyer Question Set

Create 20 questions.

Use these buckets:

- best tools
- alternatives
- comparisons
- use case questions
- buyer objection questions

Example:

- "best AI customer support software for SaaS"
- "best Zendesk alternatives for small teams"
- "Intercom vs Help Scout for startups"
- "customer support chatbot with human handoff"
- "best helpdesk software for B2B SaaS"

### Day 3: Run 3 Sample Reports

Pick 3 target companies.

For each:

- run 10 questions
- track target and competitors
- save raw answers
- summarize gaps manually

### Day 4: Create The One-Page Teaser

The teaser should show:

- 3 questions tested
- whether the target appeared
- which competitor won
- one recommended fix

This is the asset used for outreach.

### Day 5: Build Prospect List

Find 100 people:

- founders
- heads of marketing
- content leads
- SEO leads
- product marketers

Company filters:

- B2B SaaS
- has blog or resources section
- has clear competitors
- not already category leader
- likely cares about comparison pages

### Day 6-7: Send First Outreach Batch

Send 30 personalized messages.

Message:

> Hey {Name}, I’m building Bitsy.
>
> It checks whether ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini recommend your product when buyers ask category questions.
>
> I ran a small sample for {Company}. You were missing from {x}/3 answers, while {Competitor} showed up.
>
> Want me to send the 1-page version?

### Day 8-10: Deliver Free Teasers

For anyone who replies, send a small teaser.

Do not oversell.

Ask:

- Is this useful?
- Is this the kind of thing your team tracks?
- Would you want the full fix list?
- Who owns this internally?

### Day 11-14: Sell First Paid Pilots

Offer:

> I can do the full version: 10 buyer questions, 3 AI tools, competitor breakdown, raw answers, 3 recommended fixes, and one rerun after you ship a change. Early pilot price is $750.

Goal:

- 1 paid pilot
- 5 serious conversations
- 10 free teaser reports sent

## Video Strategy

The video should sell the report, not the software.

### Hook

Your buyers are asking ChatGPT who to buy from. Do you know if it recommends you or your competitor?

### Script Outline

1. Show the buyer question.
   - "What is the best AI customer support tool for SaaS?"

2. Show the problem.
   - AI recommends three competitors.
   - Your brand is missing.

3. Show Bitsy.
   - Bitsy checks the same question across ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini.

4. Show the report.
   - who appeared
   - who won
   - where you were missing

5. Show the fix list.
   - write this comparison page
   - add these proof points
   - get listed on this source

6. Show the rerun.
   - run the same questions again later
   - see what changed

7. End line.
   - "Know where AI recommends you. Know what to fix next."
