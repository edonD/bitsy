## 1. How Training Data Pipelines Work

### The CommonCrawl-to-LLM Pipeline

Every major LLM starts with CommonCrawl, the largest open web corpus. GPT-3 derived **>80% of its training tokens** from CommonCrawl; LLaMA allocated **67%** of its training mix to CommonCrawl and **4.5%** to Wikipedia ([Wikimedia Foundation, July 2023](https://wikimediafoundation.org/news/2023/07/12/wikipedias-value-in-the-age-of-generative-ai/)). At least **64% of 47 LLMs reviewed (2019-2023)** used at least one filtered version of CommonCrawl ([Springer Nature, 2025](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s00146-025-02199-9)).

The CCNet pipeline (used for LLaMA) processes raw web data through five stages ([Perone, June 2023](https://blog.christianperone.com/2023/06/appreciating-llms-data-pipelines/)):

1. **Data Source**: CommonCrawl provides WARC (raw), WAT (metadata), and WET (text-only) formats. A single snapshot (March/April 2023) is 8.7 TiB compressed in WET format alone.
2. **Deduplication**: Paragraph-level deduplication using SHA1 hashing. Text is normalized (lowercased, digits replaced, Unicode punctuation removed). **~70% of text is removed as duplicates** in the CCNet baseline. RefinedWeb uses MinHash fuzzy deduplication for even higher removal rates.
3. **Language Identification**: FastText classifier (176 languages). CCNet threshold: score >0.5. RefinedWeb uses a stricter 0.65 threshold.
4. **Quality Filtering**: CCNet trains 5-gram Kneser-Ney language models on Wikipedia, computing per-paragraph perplexity scores. Low-perplexity (Wikipedia-like) text is scored higher. RefinedWeb uses heuristic filters instead, including **a blocklist of 4.6 million domains** and URL-level word filtering.
5. **"Is-Reference" Filtering**: Additional filtering based on whether text resembles reference-quality content.

**NVIDIA's Nemotron-CC pipeline** provides the most granular public data on this process ([NVIDIA Developer Blog, 2024](https://developer.nvidia.com/blog/building-nemotron-cc-a-high-quality-trillion-token-dataset-for-llm-pretraining-from-common-crawl-using-nvidia-nemo-curator/)):
- Full CommonCrawl English dataset: **6.3 trillion tokens**
- High-quality synthetic data produced: **2 trillion tokens**
- Uses **28 distinct heuristic filters** (non-alphanumeric content, numerical/URL ratios, whitespace inconsistencies, duplicate n-gram patterns)
- An ensemble of three classifiers maps documents to 20 quality bins (0–19)
- Training with Nemotron-CC data boosted MMLU scores by **5.6 points** over the DCLM baseline (59.0 vs. 53.4)

### RLHF: How Human Feedback Shapes Recommendations

After pre-training, models are fine-tuned via Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF) in three stages ([HuggingFace RLHF Blog](https://huggingface.co/blog/rlhf)):

1. **Preference dataset creation**: Human annotators rate model outputs pairwise
2. **Reward model training**: A supervised model learns to predict human preferences
3. **RL fine-tuning**: The base LLM is optimized against the reward model using PPO

**Impact on brand mentions**: RLHF does not create explicit brand preferences but shapes models toward outputs human raters would consider helpful and safe. This means:
- Models **avoid recommending controversial brands** due to safety filters
- Positive sentiment across forums increases recommendation likelihood — the reward model penalizes outputs humans would rate poorly
- "Digital consensus" matters: when multiple authoritative sources agree on a brand's category position, RLHF-trained models adopt this as reliable information ([PageOnePower, Dec 2025](https://www.pageonepower.com/linkarati/how-do-llms-choose-which-brands-to-mention-in-results))

### The Training-to-Visibility Funnel

Content must survive four stages to influence LLM outputs ([CommonCrawl Blog](https://commoncrawl.org/blog/from-seo-to-aio-why-your-content-needs-to-exist-in-ai-training-data)):

1. **Training data acquisition** — Is the content crawled and not blocked?
2. **LLM knowledge encoding** — Does it survive deduplication and quality filtering?
3. **RAG retrieval** — Is it findable by real-time search when the LLM looks?
4. **Conversion** — Does the LLM actually cite it in its response?

**Critical blocker**: 312 of the top 10,000 domains block GPTBot entirely. Children's Hospital of Los Angeles — a top pediatric cancer center — does not appear in Gemini or ChatGPT results for leukemia treatment because Cloudflare's default `robots.txt` blocks AI crawlers. "Exclusion isn't temporary. It's essentially permanent." GPTBot traffic grew **305%** from May 2024 to May 2025.

## 2. What Makes a Company "Stick" in an LLM's Weights vs. Get Forgotten

### The Dual System: Parametric Knowledge vs. RAG

LLMs use two mechanisms to generate responses ([Metricus, 2025](https://metricusapp.com/blog/ai-visibility-how-brands-show-up/)):

1. **Parametric knowledge** (baked into weights during pre-training) — ChatGPT defaults to this for **~79% of prompts**, triggering web search only **21% of the time** (primarily for commercial/local intent queries)
2. **Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)** — Real-time web search supplements gaps. **46% of ChatGPT interactions** now use integrated search ([Onely, Dec 2025](https://www.onely.com/blog/how-chatgpt-decides-which-brands-to-recommend/))

This creates two optimization timelines:
- **Training data optimization = 18–36 month investment** (next major training cycle must include your content)
- **RAG optimization = immediate but volatile** (content can be retrieved today but may drop tomorrow)

### Academic Evidence: Frequency Drives Memorization

**"Generalization vs. Memorization" (Wang et al., ICLR 2025)** — [arxiv.org/abs/2407.14985](https://arxiv.org/abs/2407.14985):
- Evaluated Pythia models on the Pile dataset
- **Factual QA showed the strongest memorization effect** — memorization increased alongside model scaling
- Pre-training data frequency **directly correlates** with output probability distributions, especially for knowledge-retrieval scenarios
- Machine translation and reasoning tasks showed greater generalization

**"From Data to Knowledge" (Christoph et al., ACL 2025)** — [arxiv.org/abs/2506.16912](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.16912):
- **High-frequency facts**: All models perform similarly regardless of architecture
- **Low-frequency facts**: Notable performance divergence between models
- LLMs prioritize memorizing frequent facts; subsequent facts **overwrite** low-frequency memories

### What This Means for Brands

Brands frequently mentioned in high-quality content before the training cutoff become part of the model's neural weights and get mentioned **automatically, without web search**. This is the most durable form of LLM visibility.

The "substitution effect" ([MetricsRule](https://www.metricsrule.com/research/is-your-brand-ai-trainable-how-llms-decide-which-businesses-to-trust/)): When models cannot confidently retrieve lesser-known brands, they **substitute competitors with higher probabilistic weight**. Competitors literally receive citations that should have gone to absent brands.

### The Primary Recommendation Signals

**Onely's comprehensive study** ([Dec 2025](https://www.onely.com/blog/how-chatgpt-decides-which-brands-to-recommend/)) found ChatGPT's primary brand recommendation signals:

| Signal | Weight |
|--------|--------|
| Authoritative list mentions ("Best of" lists, expert roundups) | **41%** |
| Awards and accreditations | **18%** |
| Online reviews (aggregated sentiment) | **16%** |
| Traditional SEO signals (backlinks, DA, keywords) | **~0%** |

**Critical finding**: "Traditional SEO signals — backlinks, domain authority, keyword optimization — have near-zero influence on AI recommendations."

Only **3–4 brands are cited per ChatGPT response** (vs. 13 for Perplexity, ~8 for AI Overviews), creating winner-take-all dynamics. **26% of brands have zero AI visibility**. The top 50 brands capture **28.9% of all mentions**.

## 4. Role of Frequency — 1,000 Pages vs. 10 Authoritative Ones

### The Evidence: Quality-Weighted Frequency

Frequency matters, but **source quality modulates it heavily**:

> "A brand mentioned 10,000 times in low-authority blogs may score below one mentioned 200 times in peer-reviewed publications and established industry reports." — [MetricsRule](https://www.metricsrule.com/research/is-your-brand-ai-trainable-how-llms-decide-which-businesses-to-trust/)

**Specific thresholds**:
- Brands appearing **fewer than 50 times across high-trust sources** fail AI recognition **72% of the time** ([MetricsRule](https://www.metricsrule.com/research/is-your-brand-ai-trainable-how-llms-decide-which-businesses-to-trust/))
- Brands in "Best of" roundup lists are **400% more likely** to appear in LLM recommendations ([MetricsRule](https://www.metricsrule.com/research/is-your-brand-ai-trainable-how-llms-decide-which-businesses-to-trust/))
- Brands are **6.5x more likely to be cited through third-party sources** than their own domain ([MetricsRule](https://www.metricsrule.com/research/is-your-brand-ai-trainable-how-llms-decide-which-businesses-to-trust/))
- **85% of brand mentions** in AI responses come from third-party pages, not owned domains ([AirOps](https://www.airops.com/))
- Comparative listicles account for **32.5% of all AI citations** across 177 million analyzed sources ([Digital Bloom 2025 Report](https://thedigitalbloom.com/learn/2025-ai-citation-llm-visibility-report/))

### Correlation Data

**Seer Interactive** ([10,000 LLM questions analyzed](https://www.seerinteractive.com/insights/does-brand-awareness-impact-llm-visibility)):
- Brand Monthly Search Volume correlation with AI mention frequency: **0.18**
- Domain Rank correlation: **0.25** (strongest single factor)
- Backlinks: **weak or neutral** correlation

**Digital Bloom 2025 Report** ([7,000-citation analysis](https://thedigitalbloom.com/learn/2025-ai-citation-llm-visibility-report/)):
- Brand search volume: **0.334 correlation** (strongest predictor)
- Backlinks: weak/neutral
- Sites appearing on **4+ platforms** are **2.8x more likely** to appear in ChatGPT responses

### The "Patagonia Effect"

Words like "sustainable," "ethical," and "Patagonia" become **mathematically inseparable** through repeated co-occurrence in training data. When Wikipedia, Forbes, and industry blogs all agree on a brand's positioning, the LLM adopts this as fact — a phenomenon called "digital consensus" ([PageOnePower](https://www.pageonepower.com/linkarati/how-do-llms-choose-which-brands-to-mention-in-results)).

### The Noise Problem

**Excessive low-quality brand mentions dilute retrieval scores** in RAG systems by increasing noise. "Published content without third-party validation may not enter training pipelines" because CommonCrawl quality filters use classifiers trained on Reddit-upvoted content ([Stack Overflow Blog](https://stackoverflow.blog/)).

### Cross-Platform Fragmentation

Brand visibility varies **wildly** across models because each has different training data:

- Ariel detergent: **~24% Share of Model on Llama** but **<1% on Gemini** ([INSEAD](https://knowledge.insead.edu/marketing/meet-model-how-market-llms-and-sell-humans))
- Chanteclair: **19% on Perplexity** but **0% on Llama**
- Brand mentions **disagreed 62% of the time** across platforms ([SparkToro-Gumshoe study](https://sparktoro.com/))
- Only **11% of domains** are cited by both ChatGPT AND Perplexity ([Search Atlas](https://searchatlas.com/blog/comparative-analysis-of-llm-citation-behavior/))

> "Unlike search engines displaying less-popular brands on later pages, AI models are merciless. If your brand doesn't register with an LLM, it simply won't appear at all." — INSEAD/HBR

## 5. Role of Structured Data — Schema.org, JSON-LD, FAQ Pages

### The Nuanced Finding: Accuracy Yes, Frequency No

This is the most misunderstood signal in GEO. The evidence points in two directions:

**Large-scale observational study says NO frequency effect**:
- Search Atlas ([748,425 queries analyzed](https://searchatlas.com/blog/limits-of-schema-markup-for-ai-search/)): "Schema markup does not influence LLM citation frequency." "Domains with complete schema coverage perform no better than domains with minimal or no schema." Tested across OpenAI, Gemini, and Perplexity with domains categorized into five schema adoption levels (0% to 100%).

**Controlled experiments say YES quality and accuracy effect**:
- GetAISO experiment ([getaiso.com](https://www.getaiso.com/blog/schema_markup_experiment_blog_post)): Two identical fictional websites, one with schema, one without. Schema version scored **8.6/10 vs. 6.6/10** (30% improvement in response quality). Product Pricing: 6/10 → 9/10. Awards: 6/10 → 9/10. Schema-enabled responses extracted additional data (ratings: 4.8/5, 2,100+ reviews; certifications: ISO 27001, SOC 2 Type II).
- Data World study: GPT-4 goes from **16% to 54% correct responses** when content relies on structured data (38 percentage-point improvement).

**Correlation data** (observational, not causal):
- **81% of web pages cited by AI engines** included schema markup ([MetricsRule](https://www.metricsrule.com/research/is-your-brand-ai-trainable-how-llms-decide-which-businesses-to-trust/))
- ChatGPT showed **70.4% preference for Person schema** among cited sources
- Only **12.4% of registered domains** currently implement Schema.org structured data — massive opportunity gap

**Microsoft confirmation (March 2025)**: Fabrice Canel (Principal Product Manager, Bing) confirmed that "schema markup helps Microsoft's LLMs understand your content" — specifically supporting Bing Copilot AI interpretation.

### Resolution

**Schema markup improves the quality and accuracy of LLM responses about a given entity** (when they do cite you) **but does not independently increase the frequency of citation**. The citation trigger is driven by training data frequency, brand authority, third-party mentions, and content quality signals. Schema makes you "citable correctly" rather than "cited more often."

The high correlation (81% of cited pages have schema) is likely explained by confounding: sites that implement schema also tend to be higher-quality, better-maintained sites that would be cited anyway.

### Recommended Schema Types for LLM Visibility

Organization, SoftwareApplication/WebApplication, FAQPage, HowTo, Article, Review, Product, Author, Person ([Quoleady](https://www.quoleady.com/schema-structured-data-for-llm-visibility/), [Szymon Slowik](https://www.szymonslowik.com/json-ld-for-llm-seo/)).

**Important caveat** (Slowik analysis): The claim that LLMs directly "absorb" JSON-LD lacks experimental validation. **Zero peer-reviewed studies** demonstrate that models process JSON-LD differently than surrounding page text. The benefit may be indirect — structured data makes content cleaner and more parseable, which survives quality filtering better.

### How Structured Data Enters Training Pipelines

The **Web Data Commons** project specifically extracts JSON-LD from CommonCrawl, filtering billions of factual statements about products, events, and people. This creates a clean, machine-readable layer LLMs can learn from more efficiently than unstructured prose.

FAQ schema with clear question-answer pairs allows AI systems to "extract and cite those answers with high confidence" — especially for Perplexity and Google AI Overviews ([Quoleady](https://www.quoleady.com/schema-structured-data-for-llm-visibility/)).

## 6. The GEO Paper: What Content Features Increase LLM Visibility

### "GEO: Generative Engine Optimization" (Princeton/Georgia Tech/IIT Delhi)

**Paper**: Aggarwal, Murahari, Rajpurohit, Kalyan, Narasimhan, Deshpande — accepted at **ACM SIGKDD 2024** — [arxiv.org/abs/2311.09735](https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.09735)

**Methodology**: GEO-bench benchmark of 10,000 queries (8K train/1K validation/1K test) sourced from nine datasets (MS MARCO, ORCAS-I, Natural Questions, AllSouls, LIMA, Davinci-Debate, Perplexity Discover, ELI5, GPT-4 generated). 25 domains, 80% informational queries.

**Nine strategies tested** (Position-Adjusted Word Count metric):

| Strategy | Visibility Change |
|----------|------------------|
| Quotation Addition | **+41%** |
| Statistics Addition | **+37%** |
| Cite Sources | **+30%** (up to +115.1% for rank-5 sites) |
| Fluency Optimization | **+28%** |
| Authoritative Tone | Moderate (domain-dependent) |
| Technical Terms | Moderate |
| Easy-to-Understand | Modest |
| Unique Words | Minimal |
| Keyword Stuffing | **-10%** (negative on Perplexity) |

**Real-world validation on Perplexity.ai**: Quotation Addition +22%, Statistics Addition +37%, Keyword Stuffing -10%.

**Democratization effect**: Lower-ranked websites benefit disproportionately. Rank-5 sites saw **+115.1%** improvement with Cite Sources; rank-1 sites saw **-30.3%** (competitive displacement).

**Best combination**: Fluency Optimization + Statistics Addition exceeded any single method by **5.5%**. Cite Sources paired with other methods averages **31.4% improvement**.

**Domain-specific effectiveness**:
- Debate/History: Authoritative tone
- Factual/Law/Government: Cite Sources and Statistics
- People & Society: Quotation Addition
- Business/Health: Fluency Optimization

### Additional Content Optimization Evidence

**Digital Bloom 2025 Report** ([thedigitalbloom.com](https://thedigitalbloom.com/learn/2025-ai-citation-llm-visibility-report/)):
- Comparative listicles: **32.5% of AI citations** (highest format)
- Opinion blogs: 9.91%
- Product/service descriptions: 4.73%
- Self-contained chunks of **40–60 words**: optimal for AI extraction (0.648 accuracy, lowest variance — NVIDIA research)
- Comparison tables: **47% higher AI citation rates**

**Genezio study** ([2,909 citations across 946 queries](https://genezio.com/blog/content-types-that-drive-llm-mentions/)): Six content types that drive LLM mentions:
1. High-authority informational sources
2. Rankings and structured comparative guides (highest influence)
3. Program/product pages with clear structure
4. Outcomes, metrics, and evidence-based content
5. Intent-aligned content (matching user phrasing patterns)
6. Citation footprint and external coverage

## 7. The Stochasticity Problem: LLMs Are Not Deterministic Recommenders

### "LLM Whisperer" (Carnegie Mellon / Center for AI Safety)

**Paper**: Lin, Gerchanovsky, Akgul, Bauer, Fredrikson, Wang — [arxiv.org/abs/2406.04755](https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.04755) — June 2024, revised Feb 2025.

449 prompts across 77 product categories, 1,000 responses per prompt, four LLMs tested.

**Key findings**:
- Subtle synonym replacements can increase brand mention likelihood by up to **78%**
- Semantically equivalent (paraphrased) prompts produced absolute mention differences of **7.4% to 18.6%** on average
- Maximum variance: **100%** (e.g., InstantPot mentions shifted from 0% to 100% between equivalent pressure cooker prompts)
- Human subjects (334 participants) could not distinguish altered prompts from originals
- Attack effectiveness correlated with baseline brand mention frequency — higher pre-existing mentions enable larger perturbation effects

**Implication**: LLM brand recommendations are partly **stochastic language patterns** rather than reasoned decisions. This means monitoring must be statistical (many samples) rather than spot-checking.

### Cross-Platform Inconsistency

- Brand mentions **disagreed 62% of the time** across platforms ([SparkToro-Gumshoe](https://sparktoro.com/))
- Less than **1-in-100 chance** of any platform producing the same recommendation list twice ([Metricus](https://metricusapp.com/ai-platform-comparison-brands/))
- **40–60% of cited sources change month-to-month** on tracked prompts ([Semrush AI Visibility Index](https://searchengineland.com/what-is-generative-engine-optimization-geo-444418))

## 9. Platform-Specific Behaviors

### ChatGPT
- **47.9% of citations from Wikipedia**, 11.3% from Reddit ([Digital Bloom](https://thedigitalbloom.com/learn/2025-ai-citation-llm-visibility-report/))
- Defaults to parametric knowledge; triggers web search only **21%** of the time
- **87% of SearchGPT citations** match Bing's top 10 organic results
- **2.37 brand mentions** average per response
- **3.2x** more often mentions brands than actually cites them (mentions > citations)
- GPT-5.4 knowledge cutoff: August 2025
- Market share: **68% of AI chatbot market**
- Launched ads February 2026 at **$60 CPM, $200K minimum**

### Perplexity
- Reddit leads at **46.7% of top 10 citations**, YouTube 13.9%, Gartner 7.0%
- Indexes **200B+ URLs**
- **92% citation integration rate**; typical response includes 5–10 inline citations
- Performs live web search for **every query**
- Most citations occur within **2–3 days of publication** (up to 2%), decaying to 0.5% within 1–2 months
- Abandoned advertising entirely in February 2026

## 12. Implications for Bitsy

### What Bitsy Should Build

1. **Multi-model polling is non-negotiable.** Brand mentions disagree 62% of the time across platforms. Monitoring a single model gives a misleading picture. Bitsy must poll ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and Claude — at minimum.

2. **Statistical sampling is essential.** The LLM Whisperer paper (CMU) proved that identical-intent prompts produce wildly different brand mentions. Tryscope's approach of **50 samples/day** is a reasonable minimum for confidence. Single-shot queries are meaningless.

3. **Track parametric vs. RAG mentions separately.** ChatGPT uses parametric knowledge 79% of the time. A brand that appears in parametric responses has durable visibility; one that only appears via RAG is volatile. Bitsy should detect which mechanism is driving each mention (e.g., by testing with web search disabled vs. enabled where APIs allow).

4. **Recency scoring is a feature.** Content freshness is one of the strongest signals (65% of AI hits on <1 year content). Bitsy should track when a brand's cited sources were published and alert when they're aging out.

5. **Third-party monitoring > owned content monitoring.** 85% of brand mentions come from third-party pages. Bitsy should monitor where the brand appears in "Best of" lists, comparison articles, review sites, and Wikipedia — not just the brand's own website.

6. **The cost calculator should model both parametric and RAG timelines.** Training data optimization is an 18–36 month investment; RAG optimization is immediate but volatile. Different pricing for different goals.

### What Bitsy Should NOT Build

1. **Do not build a keyword-stuffing optimizer.** The GEO paper proved keyword stuffing has a **negative** impact (-10% on Perplexity). Any tool that encourages keyword density optimization for AI search is selling snake oil.

2. **Do not promise deterministic results.** Less than 1-in-100 chance of any platform producing the same recommendation list twice. 40–60% of cited sources change month-to-month. Bitsy should set expectations about variance, not promise "guaranteed" visibility improvements.

3. **Do not treat all models as interchangeable.** Ariel detergent has ~24% share on Llama but <1% on Gemini. Each model has different training data, different retrieval mechanisms, and different citation behaviors. A "total GEO score" across models would be misleading — per-model scoring is essential.

4. **Do not ignore the backlink myth.** Many potential customers will expect backlinks to matter (because they matter in SEO). Bitsy should explicitly show that backlinks have weak/neutral correlation with AI mentions, educating users away from wasted effort.

### Architectural Recommendations

- **Prompt variation engine**: Generate 5–10 semantically equivalent phrasings per query to account for stochastic variation (inspired by LLM Whisperer findings).
- **Source attribution parser**: For each brand mention, attempt to identify whether it came from parametric knowledge or RAG (test with/without search).
- **Freshness tracker**: Monitor the publication dates of sources cited alongside the target brand; alert when median citation age exceeds 12 months.
- **"Best of" list tracker**: Crawl authoritative "Best of" / comparison articles in the brand's category and flag when competitors appear but the target brand doesn't.
- **Schema audit**: Check if the brand's website implements recommended schema types (Organization, FAQPage, Product, Review) and compare to competitors.
- **Wikipedia presence check**: Verify whether the brand has a Wikipedia article; flag if competitors do and the brand doesn't.
