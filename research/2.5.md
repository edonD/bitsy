# 2.5 User Complaints, Reviews & Market Gaps in GEO/LLMO Tools

> Research task: Search Reddit, Twitter/X, G2, Product Hunt, blogs, and LinkedIn for user complaints, reviews, and feedback about GEO/LLMO tools. Focus on what users complain about, what features are missing, what's overpriced, what doesn't work well, and where the gaps in the market are.

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The GEO/LLMO tools market is less than two years old and deeply dysfunctional. The category raised $77M+ in collective funding between May-August 2025 alone, with Profound reaching a $1B unicorn valuation in February 2026. Yet users consistently report: tools that monitor without prescribing action, hallucination-blind dashboards, prompt-based pricing that punishes scale, repackaged SEO features sold under new acronyms, and a fundamental inability to measure what actually matters. The most damning finding: out of 11 AI visibility tools tested, only ONE consistently detected when AI was describing brands incorrectly. The rest tracked "popularity" while ignoring factual accuracy -- the thing that actually damages conversions.

---

## SECTION 1: UNIVERSAL COMPLAINTS (ACROSS ALL TOOLS)

### 1.1 Monitoring Without Actionable Guidance

The single most consistent complaint across the entire category. Users say: **"Don't just tell me I'm not showing up -- tell me what to fix."**

- Profound: "doesn't tell you why competitors rank higher or provide specific actions to improve" — provides "comprehensive visibility into what's happening without a roadmap for what to do" (Source: [Gauge comparison](https://www.withgauge.com/resources/best-profound-alternatives))
- Otterly: "No competitive gap analysis or strategic recommendations" (Source: [Gauge comparison](https://www.withgauge.com/resources/best-profound-alternatives))
- Ahrefs Brand Radar: "monitoring only — no optimization guidance" (Source: [Geoptie](https://geoptie.com/blog/best-geo-tools))
- Semrush AI Toolkit: "It tells you that you're invisible to AI engines, but it offers limited workflows to fix it" (Source: [Getmint](https://getmint.ai/resources/semrush-review))
- A commenter on r/b2bmarketing: "What's been more useful for us is combining classic SEO monitoring with structured AI tracking instead of treating them as separate universes."

### 1.2 Hallucination Blindness -- The Critical Gap

**90% of AI visibility tools track mentions while only 10% detect accuracy and hallucinations.** (Source: [LLMClicks](https://llmclicks.ai/blog/top-ai-visibility-tracker-tools/))

Specific test results:
- A tester found their brand mentioned in 18 AI responses. "4 had outdated pricing, 3 confused our features with a competitor's, 2 recommended us for use cases we don't support, 1 hallucinated an integration that doesn't exist."
- **Otterly.ai** "showed our brand mentioned in 18 prompts. It marked all 18 as positive. When manually checked, 4 contained wrong information. Otterly caught none of them."
- **Peec AI:** "I found mentions Peec marked positive sentiment that contained outdated pricing information."
- **Profound:** "Despite sophistication, Profound tracks mentions and positioning but not accuracy."

Quote: "If AI invents features or gets your pricing wrong, Otterly won't tell you. You have to manually review every mention."

### 1.3 API vs. Real-UI Data Disconnect

Most tools rely on API-based data that "can produce different results than what real users see in the chat interface." Real users access ChatGPT's web interface, but "API-based tools can return different results because they bypass the retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) layer that pulls in web sources." (Source: [Metricus](https://metricusapp.com/blog/ai-visibility-tools-worth-it-2026/))

This means the data these tools report may not reflect what actual users see when they ask ChatGPT a question.

### 1.4 Non-Deterministic Responses Make Tracking Unreliable

Lily Ray (prominent SEO expert) identifies: "Unlike standardized search results, LLM outputs vary based on conversation history, user memory settings, interests, and geographic nuances." Third-party scrapers cannot replicate personalization layers. Any prompt volume metrics are "at best, highly directional and greatly sampled." (Source: [Lily Ray Substack](https://lilyraynyc.substack.com/p/a-reflection-on-seo-and-ai-search))

### 1.5 Prompt-Based Pricing That Punishes Scale

Nearly every tool uses prompt/credit-based pricing that becomes expensive at scale:
- **Otterly:** "prompt-based pricing model...can become expensive quickly — if you're tracking 50 keywords across 3 competitors, you'll burn through your prompt budget fast" ($29-$489/month)
- **BrightEdge:** "credit-based pricing model can become expensive quickly because the system uses one credit per keyword per SERP"
- **AthenaHQ:** "uses a credit-based system, and as you enable more prompts and AI answer engines, the credits (and costs) add up"
- **Rankscale:** "credits burn fast at scale"
- **Semrush:** "$99/mo per additional domain; prompt limits can be restrictive"

### 1.6 Source Attribution Is Missing or Limited

"Source attribution — showing you the specific URLs that feed an AI model's answer — is either absent or limited across most platforms." Teams can't identify which sources are creating factual errors about their brands. (Source: [Metricus](https://metricusapp.com/blog/ai-visibility-tools-worth-it-2026/))

### 1.7 No Historical Data / Limited Trending

"Only 30% of brands remain visible across consecutive snapshots" -- yet many platforms lack historical data, leaving teams "flying blind." (Source: [AuthorityTech](https://authoritytech.io/blog/ai-visibility-tracking-tools-2026-market-guide))

40-60% of cited sources change month-to-month across Google AI Mode and ChatGPT, making visibility far less stable than organic search rankings.

### 1.8 The Repackaging Problem

"Most offerings are repackaged SEO tools with surface-level AI features, not the deep Machine Relations measurement frameworks enterprises actually need." (Source: [AuthorityTech](https://authoritytech.io/blog/ai-visibility-tracking-tools-2026-market-guide))

Google's John Mueller (August 14, 2025): **"The higher the urgency, and the stronger the push of new acronyms, the more likely they're just making spam and scamming."**

Lily Ray: "Many GEO grifters were using this opportunity to simply repackage core SEO approaches using a different name." (Source: [Lily Ray Substack](https://lilyraynyc.substack.com/p/a-reflection-on-seo-and-ai-search))

## SECTION 2: TOOL-SPECIFIC COMPLAINTS

### 2.1 Profound ($499-$5,000+/month)

**G2 Rating:** 4.6/5 across 140+ reviews

**Pricing Complaints:**
- Lite plan ($499/month) only tracks ChatGPT with 50 prompts and a single seat
- Growth plan ($399/month) adds Perplexity and Google AI Overviews
- Full platform coverage, API access, and unlimited seats locked behind Enterprise at $2,000-$5,000+/month
- "$99/mo Starter seen as a deliberate funnel toward the $399/mo Growth plan — 50 prompts on ChatGPT only is enough to get oriented, but not enough to run a real AEO program"

**Feature Complaints:**
- "Recommended actions (which are mostly 'write a generic listicle') prove somewhat useless" (Source: [Alex Birkett](https://alexbirkett.com/generative-engine-optimization-software/))
- "Prompt volume" tracking is "somewhat useless at this stage" per Alex Birkett
- Interface "is a bit noisy and there are superfluous features"
- "Requires a formal application process. You can't test the platform before committing"
- Data-heavy dashboard feels "overwhelming"; difficulty exporting data for reports

**What Users Like:**
- Prompt Volumes (proprietary panel data showing what real people ask AI) -- "keyword research for AI search" that no other tool offers at scale
- Strong enterprise security (SOC 2 compliance)

Sources: [Getmint](https://getmint.ai/resources/profound-review), [Aipeekaboo](https://www.aipeekaboo.com/blog/profound-review), [Trakkr](https://trakkr.ai/reviews/profound-review), [Writesonic](https://writesonic.com/blog/profound-review)

## SECTION 3: MARKET-LEVEL GAPS & STRUCTURAL PROBLEMS

### 3.1 The Measurement Crisis

What remains currently unmeasurable:
- **Prompt volume:** AI platforms don't share query data
- **Why content gets cited:** LLMs are opaque about selection criteria
- **Individual source weight:** When answers blend multiple sources, contribution is unknowable
- **AI traffic attribution:** Users who get AI recommendations then search Google are misattributed as organic traffic

The 1.08% AI referral traffic figure from Conductor "represents a floor rather than a ceiling for actual AI-influenced traffic" because analytics platforms misattribute AI-influenced sessions.

### 3.2 The Citation-to-Traffic Paradox

- Sites receive **less than 1% of referral traffic** from AI platforms despite being frequently cited
- **93% of AI search sessions end without a website click**
- AI Overviews reduce average CTR for top-ranking pages by **58%** (Ahrefs data)
- "If your brand isn't cited inside the answer, you're absent from the discovery journey entirely"

### 3.3 Tool Oversaturation & Unsustainability

- Lily Ray: "We are now in a situation where there are far too many AI search tools circulating in our industry, and not enough users to pay for all of them."
- "Vibe coding" phenomenon: Cursor, Claude Code, and Lovable helped non-developers generate AI search tracking tools in hours
- **Lorelight** (GEO platform tracking brand mentions) shut down October 31, 2025
- **GummySearch** (Reddit monitoring tool) shut down December 2025 after Reddit denied API access -- "thousands of users losing saved searches, historical data, and workflows overnight"
- Incumbents (Semrush, Ahrefs, Conductor) have structural advantages: existing contracts, security clearances, established relationships

### 3.4 The GEO Disinformation Problem

- Emerging GEO tools allegedly **paid micro-influencers** to promote narratives about SEO's obsolescence
- A Reddit user (weblinker) documented "organized sponsored promoted campaigns of disinformation being led by a number of GEO tools OEMs" on r/SEO
- Ann Smarty and 75+ marketers debated whether "GEO" is even a useful term or just rebranded SEO
- Only 43% of industry leaders include "SEO" in LinkedIn headlines vs. 3% for "GEO"
- **Fewer than one-third** of 75 SEO thought leaders maintained consistent GEO sentiment and terminology over 2025

### 3.5 GEO Myths That Don't Hold Up

Per Search Engine Land's investigation:
- **llms.txt files:** "There is no data or evidence showing that llms.txt files boost AI inclusion"
- **Schema markup for AI:** "No solid evidence exists that AI chatbots access schema markup during retrieval" -- Perplexity actually hallucinated schema markup on pages where it didn't exist
- **Fresh content priority:** Has the strongest evidence but studies used "highly artificial" date injection methods
- Most GEO recommendations "fail at the evidence-vs-proof stage" on the ladder of inference

Source: [Search Engine Land](https://searchengineland.com/geo-myths-lies-467617)

### 3.6 The Scam Economy

A Toronto e-commerce company **paid $50,000** for "Generative Engine Optimization expertise" -- received **zero ChatGPT citations and zero AI Overview appearances after six months.**

Reality vs. marketing claims:
| Metric | Reality | Marketing Claims |
|--------|---------|------------------|
| ChatGPT traffic share | 0.19% of web traffic | 4.33% "search market share" |
| AI search overall | <1% of website traffic | "Future-proofing visibility" |
| Growth rate context | 1,200% from tiny baseline | Presented without context |

Source: [The Ad Spend](https://theadspend.com/resources/ai-search-scam)

## SECTION 5: WHAT USERS ACTUALLY WANT (THE GAPS)

Based on all complaints and reviews analyzed:

1. **Accuracy verification, not just mention counting** -- Tell me if what AI says about my brand is CORRECT, not just that it mentioned me
2. **Actionable remediation** -- "Don't just tell me I'm not showing up -- tell me what to fix" with specific content changes
3. **Affordable multi-platform coverage** -- Track ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity, Copilot, DeepSeek without paying $500+/month
4. **Real-UI data, not API approximations** -- Show what actual users see, not what the API returns
5. **Revenue/ROI attribution** -- Connect GEO efforts to actual business outcomes, not vanity metrics
6. **Competitive gap analysis** -- Show WHY competitors rank higher and WHAT specific actions to take
7. **Historical trending with low latency** -- Daily (not weekly) updates with longitudinal data
8. **Transparent scoring methodology** -- Explain how visibility scores are calculated so users can optimize
9. **Workflow integration** -- Connect to GA4, Search Console, CMS platforms, and existing SEO tools
10. **Honest pricing** -- Not credit/prompt traps that force upgrades; predictable costs at scale
